Your Metabolic Type

The Stressed StriverCortisol is running — and ruining — your results.

You're driven, hardworking, and you pour everything into your career, your kids, and everyone else around you. But your body is paying a price. When stress is chronic, cortisol — your body's main stress hormone — stays elevated around the clock. And elevated cortisol tells your body to store fat, especially around your stomach, while draining your energy and making fat loss feel impossible no matter what you do.

Skipping meals during the day, relying on caffeine to push through, and collapsing at night isn't just exhausting — it's actively signaling your body to hold on to every calorie it can. Your metabolism isn't slow because you're not trying hard enough. It's slow because your nervous system has been running a marathon with no finish line in sight.

✦ Your Action This Week
Commit to eating within 90 minutes of waking up every single day this week — even if it's small. A protein-rich breakfast (aim for 30g+) before your first coffee will stabilize your cortisol curve and reduce that 2-3pm crash. Do this for 7 days and notice the difference in your afternoon energy.
